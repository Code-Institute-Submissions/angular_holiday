Beach and water activities

For such a tiny island there is no shortage of beaches. We’ve listed a few here to give you an idea of the varitey Montserrat has to offer.

Little Bay – The most popular beach on island for residents and visitors. Little Bay is a great spot to watch boats coming and going from the nearby port, enjoy a swim, or unwind with a drink at one of numerous beachside bars.

Isle Bay Beach/Old Road Bay – We have the volcano flows to thank for this extension of the beach. Take a trek from one end of the beach to the next and enjoy seeing how nature has destroyed and remade this lovely bit of coastline.

Rendezvous Beach – Montserrat’s only white sand beach can be reached via a short boat ride from Little Bay or by a pretty intense one-hour hike. The reward is having a nice swim in a very secluded spot.

Scuba diving

Montserrat is home to several top quality dive schools that offer dive trainig tours. Scuba divers here can enjoy uncrowded dives in pristiene conditions. Most dives are less than 60 feet deep and typical visibilaty is 80 feet.

Snorkeling

Snorkeling enthusiasts often note the exquisite beauty of the coral reefs that are well-preserved and rich in Montserrat. Explore the warm Caribbean water, temperature on average is 28ºC / 82ºF.

Spear fishing

Swim alongside trained experts as you get up close and personal with your targets. Using elastic powered spear guns combine the fun of snorkelling with the challenge of underwater fishing. while perfecting your shooting technique as you hunt and catch your dinner.

Shoreline fishing

Take in your surroundings as you relax on the shore, drink in hand, a bucket full of sprat and patiently wait for that tug at your line to reel the big one in!

Kayack and paddle board

he calm caribbean sea and dramatic cliffs rising out of the water make kayack tours an amazing experience while in Montserrat.

Island Tours

Explore the lush rainforest, Centre Hills with a knowledgeable guide and take in views of the land, sea and coastline. A chance to see the national bird, the Montserrat oriole, the shy bridled quail dove, the mangrove cuckoo and the trembler and purple throated carib to name a few.

Volcano Tours

The Soufriere Hills Volcano is one of the worlds only active volcano’s that you can observe at close quarters safely. What was once the capital of Montserrat, Plymouth, now appears as a scattering of roofs and the top floors of the tallest buildings, just poking out from a landscape of ash and boulders. In what has been called a modern day Pompeii.

Wildlife

Birds

Montserrat is a bird lovers paradise. 117 different bird species have been recored on the island some of which can be seen on the nature trails.

Bats

There are a number of species of bats which can be seen in there natural habitat by swimming to a cave they reside in. Snorkelers and divers alike can access the cave by swimming from a nearby beach or more directly from a boat.

Marine Life

Marine life is abundant in the waters round Montserrat. There are hawksbill turtles, puffer fish, blennie, golden tail eel, spotted snake eel, sharp tail eel, squid, sea horse, sea turtles, flying gurnard, southern stingray, squid, angelfish, peacock flounder… and more. Dolphins, eagle rays, nurse sharks and pipefish are also seen in the waters.

Hikes

There are a number of world-class hiking trails available to hikers in Montserrat. The hikes can be quite difficult but a great adventure. Hikers recently found ancient stone carvings dating back 1000 - 1500 years. Some hikes include visiting the ruins of abandond villages as well as long forgotten banana plantations that still bare fruit.

Rum shop tour

One of the best ways to meet and mingle with the locals. Our rum shop tour is exclusive to emerald travel guests. Try the famous rum punch or enjoy any of the wide range in stocked drinks on the island.

Culture and food

The best way to explore the island’s culture, food and people is to travel trough the various parishes for a drink, eat or chill with the locals at the various rum shops restaurants and cafes across the island.

Food and drink

There is an abundamce of restaurants and bars offering everything from international cuisine to local specialties.

Goat water, the national dish of Montserrat is a delicious hearty stew served with crusty bread

Some other local specialties include: Saltfish, pumpkin soup and mountain chicken (frogs legs).

Music

Soca music is the national favourtie and can be heard playing in many of the islands rum shops, bars cafe’s and clubs

Past pop stars include the soca bandleader Alphonsus "Arrow" Cassell, known for 1983's worldwide hit "Hot! Hot! Hot!". Air Montserrat, the recording studio founded by Beatles producer Sir George Martin saw artists such as Sting, Eric Clapton, Paul McCartney, Elton John, Michael Jackson, Stevie Wonder, the Rolling Stones, Dire Straits and Lou Reed record there.

Calypso music is also popular, as are the vocal choirs Voices and the Emerald Community Singers are well known throughout the island.

Masquerade

The masqurade plays a large part of any celebration the Island has. Deeply rooted in the African culture, the colourful costumes and creative desines are a marvel to see.
